Your GNOME base is now working, but the remaining problem is that **Dash-to-Dock is only an app dock**. It will not become your full Windows/Ubuntu-style bottom control bar with battery, Wi-Fi, sound, clock, app launcher, and running-app indicators. For that exact layout, the better GNOME-native layer is **Dash-to-Panel**, because it combines the GNOME dash, running apps, system tray, and panel indicators into one bottom panel. That matches your original taskbar/dock requirement better than Dash-to-Dock. ([GNOME Extensions][1])

GNOME’s wallpaper renderer supports `scaled`, `zoom`, `centered`, `stretched`, and related modes, and it also supports a primary background colour. For your requirement, we should use `scaled` plus black background colour. That means portrait/mobile wallpapers fit vertically with black side bars, and non-matching desktop wallpapers fit while leaving black bars where needed. ([GNOME Help][2])

One design correction: **battery, Wi-Fi, sound, and clock cannot be inserted into Dash-to-Dock itself** without writing a custom GNOME Shell extension. Dash-to-Panel is the correct production-level GNOME extension for that layout because it explicitly combines the app launcher/taskbar and system tray into one panel. ([GNOME Extensions][1])
